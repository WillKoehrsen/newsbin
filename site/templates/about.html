newsbin

newsbin pulls articles from hundreds of rss feeds provided by major news outlets, and fetches the articles into a single, combined stream. The feed is as up-to-date as it can be without spamming requests at the providers, which works out to about a 10 minute delay.

You can uses the tools in the left side to fetch the news that interests you- by source, category or keyword.

While reading an article, you'll notice that some of the names and places are clickable. If you click on them, you'll get a brief summary of the topic without leaving the article. These are annotations. If you want to add more, just copy and paste a word or phrase (one that has an article on wikipedia) into the box at the top-left of the page and click 'Add'. Newsbin will fetch the information and it will be highlighted in every article from then on.

If you see an annotation that's wrong, you can blacklist it for the current article by copying the text into the submission box and clicking 'Remove.'

If you want to see what features are currently under development, you can take a look at open issues on github, and if you want to contribute, you can send me an email.
